## Limitations (important)
- The authoritative registries — **EUIPO eSearch plus**, **TMview**, and the
  **Danish PVS (DKPTO)** database — **block automated access**, so this is a
  web-level sweep, **not** a registry-level clearance.
- Trademark conflicts hinge on **confusing similarity within the relevant
  class**, not just identical names. For an app the relevant classes are likely
  **Nice class 9** (software) and **class 41/44** (fitness/health services).
  Marks like *Walkly* / *Walkr* / *Walkify* in class 9 *could* be raised by an
  examiner or opponent, though "Walkful" (the "-ful / mindful" suffix) is
  phonetically and conceptually distinct.

## Recommended next steps (owner)
1. Run the definitive search yourself:
   - **EUIPO eSearch plus / TMview** — <https://www.tmdn.org/tmview/> — search
     "walkful" and the "walk*" stem, filter to classes 9 / 41 / 44.
   - **Danish PVS** — <https://www.dkpto.dk/> trademark database.
2. If clear and you want protection, consider filing an **EU trade mark (EUTM)**
   via EUIPO in class 9 (+ 41/44) — one EUTM covers all EU states incl. Denmark.
3. For anything borderline, get a short clearance opinion from an IP advisor
   before launch spend.

## Bottom line
No identical "Walkful" mark or app was found anywhere searchable — a good sign
the name is available. Treat this as a green-light **to proceed with the
registry search and (optional) filing**, not as legal confirmation on its own.
