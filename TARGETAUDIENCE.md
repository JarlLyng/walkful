# Walkful — Target Audience

> Who we build and sell Walkful for. Use this to focus the product, the App
> Store listing, the marketing site, and ad/keyword targeting. Revisit when
> reviews, analytics, or sales tell us something new.

---

## 1. The one-sentence ICP

**Privacy-minded, Apple-ecosystem adults (≈30–65) who want to move more for their long-term health — and who are tired of subscriptions, ads, data-harvesting and streak-guilt.**

Walkful sits at the intersection of three rising, paying audiences:

1. **The Apple ecosystem** — affluent, health-active iPhone + Apple Watch owners.
2. **The privacy backlash** — people who will *pay* to not be the product.
3. **The longevity / "every step counts" movement** — mainstreamed by the 2025 Lancet 7,000-steps science.

Our wedge against the field (Pacer, StepsApp, Fitbit, Pedometer++): **one-time purchase, no ads, nothing leaves the device, calm and evidence-based.** Every competitor leans on subscriptions, ads, social pressure, or data. We are the deliberate opposite.

---

## 2. Why now (market context)

- **Subscription & data fatigue is real and monetisable.** 52% of consumers would pay more for brands they trust with their data, and 29% would *rather pay than share their data* at all.¹ Over 90% of US online adults now use at least one privacy tool.¹ The privacy-software market is growing ~30%/yr.¹
- **The Apple wearable base is large, wealthy and health-driven.** 31% of people earning $75k+ use a smartwatch/tracker vs 12% of those under $30k; 83% of smartwatch owners use health/fitness features regularly.² Roughly 1 in 3 iPhone owners also owns an Apple Watch.²
- **The longevity economy is enormous and older-skewed.** US 50+ spending: ~$8.3T (2018) → projected $13.5T (2032); the longevity-focused fitness/wellness market is heading toward ~$50B by 2030; 70%+ of consumers say they've increased longevity/wellness spending.³ People 60+ use fitness apps *more* than 18–44s (74.5% vs 46%).³
- **The 7,000-step finding went mainstream.** The Lancet 2025 dose-response meta-analysis (≈7,000 steps → 50–70% lower all-cause mortality vs ~2,000) reframed "how much should I walk?" away from the 10,000 myth.⁴ Walkful is built natively around this story.
- **Gamification is burning people out.** Users report anxiety, guilt and self-criticism over broken streaks; many abandon gamified health apps from "technological exhaustion."⁵ This is a tailwind for *calm* tracking.

## 3. Primary personas

### A. "Mette / Mark — the Privacy-First Professional" (core buyer)
- **Age 30–50**, urban/suburban, above-median income, iPhone + (often) Apple Watch.
- Tech-literate enough to *care* that most free health apps sell data. Runs a VPN or ad-blocker, uses Apple's "Hide My Email," reflexively distrusts "free."
- **Hates subscriptions** for simple utilities; happily pays once for software that respects them.
- Walks to decompress and stay healthy; doesn't want a coach yelling or a leaderboard judging.
- **What converts them:** "Data Not Collected", "one-time purchase, no subscription", "nothing leaves your device."
- **Where they are:** Apple App Store search, privacy communities (r/privacy, PrivacyGuides, Mastodon), Apple-focused press/blogs, word-of-mouth.

### B. "Bente — the Longevity-Minded Midlifer" (core buyer, highest LTV potential)
- **Age 45–65**, health-conscious, reads about healthspan/aging-well, may have had a "wake-up" health moment.
- Walking is their **sustainable** exercise — no gym, no lycra, no joints-destroying running.
- Wants **meaning, not vanity metrics**: "what does this number do for me?" The 7,000-step science is genuinely motivating to them.
- Has disposable income and spends on health; values credible, non-hype sources (Bente Klarlund Pedersen, Lancet).
- **What converts them:** the science narrative, the calm dashboard, the "7,000 not 10,000" reframing, honest "associated with" claims.
- **Where they are:** health/longevity newsletters & podcasts, Facebook health groups, Google searches for "how many steps per day", GEO answers (ChatGPT/Perplexity "best private walking app").

### C. "the Calm Seeker — refugee from streak-pressure apps" (cross-cutting)
- Any age; **burned out** by Fitbit/Strava/Apple-rings guilt, competitive friends, and "you've been sitting too long!" nagging (a real, common complaint about rivals like StepsApp).
- Wants gentle, optional, non-judgmental tracking. Mental-health-adjacent motivation: walking as self-care.
- **What converts them:** "compete with yourself, no leaderboards", "gentle nudges only when you've actually been still", "calm, no manipulation."

## 4. Secondary / expansion segments

- **Active-aging 60+** — fastest-growing fitness-app cohort and cash-rich, but UX-sensitive. Walkful's **Dynamic Type, VoiceOver and high-contrast** support makes it credibly usable here; lean into "active aging," never "senior." Apple Watch fall/heart features pull them into the ecosystem; we ride alongside.
- **Recovery / "doctor told me to walk more"** — post-illness, pregnancy, desk-bound knowledge workers told to reduce sedentary time. They want a low-pressure on-ramp, not athletic tooling.
- **Quantified-but-private** — people who *like* data (heatmaps, trends, records, CSV export) but refuse cloud accounts. Walkful's Pro Insights + on-device export is built for them.
- **The IAMJARL halo** — existing users of your other apps who already trust the "private, one-time, made-by-human" brand. Cheapest acquisition you have; cross-promote.

---

## 5. Anti-personas (who we deliberately do NOT chase)

Saying no here keeps the product focused and the reviews high.

- **Competitive athletes / Strava crowd** — want pace, segments, social ranking, training load. Not us.
- **Gamification/rewards seekers** — "walk-to-earn", coins, badges, leaderboards. Against our ethos and our on-device architecture.
- **Weight-loss-at-all-costs / 10k-or-bust** — we actively reframe away from this.
- **Android-only users** — we're iOS/Apple Health by design (for now).
- **People who want a social network / friends & challenges** — we have no accounts or servers, on purpose.
- **Free-only users who won't pay anything** — fine as free-tier users, but not our buyer; we won't add ads or data-sale to monetise them.

---

## 6. Shared values (what all our buyers have in common)

This is the emotional core to hit in every asset:

1. **"Respect me."** No ads, no data sale, no dark patterns, no subscription trap.
2. **"Don't stress me."** Calm, optional, guilt-free. Progress, not pressure.
3. **"Tell me the truth."** Evidence-based, hedged honestly ("associated with"), not fitness marketing.
4. **"Keep it mine."** My data, my phone, my pace, my records.
5. **"Make it beautiful and effortless."** Premium Apple-native feel (Aurora), zero setup, just works.

## 8. Channels & GEO/SEO priorities

- **App Store (ASO)** is the primary funnel — optimise keywords around *private/offline step counter, walking tracker no subscription, pedometer no ads*. (See `docs/app-store-release.md`, issue #37.)
- **Generative engines (GEO)** — people increasingly ask ChatGPT/Perplexity "best private walking app" or "how many steps should I walk." Our site's FAQ/Organization schema and the 7,000-step content target exactly these answers. Keep feeding it (issue #39: walking-science articles).
- **Privacy communities** — earned/organic, high-trust, low-cost.
- **Longevity/health content** — newsletters, podcasts, Facebook groups; the science angle travels.
- **Danish / Nordic beachhead** — privacy-friendly culture, the maker's home market, and a localised site (`/da/`) already exists. Use it to seed reviews and word-of-mouth before scaling internationally (English).

---

## 9. Product implications (what this audience means for the roadmap)

- **Protect the wedge.** Never add ads, accounts, social, or a subscription. These would betray every primary persona at once.
- **Calm > engagement-maxxing.** No streak-shaming, no manipulative notifications. "Gentle" is a feature, not a limitation.
- **Make the science legible.** Meaning line, longevity-zone card, honest hedging — this is our differentiation, lean in (carefully, never medical claims).
- **Accessibility = market access.** Dynamic Type/VoiceOver isn't just ethics; it unlocks the cash-rich 60+ segment.
- **Pro must feel "worth owning, forever."** Insights, records, CSV export, coach — depth that justifies a one-time price to data-savvy users. Avoid anything that needs a server.
- **iOS-first, deliberately.** Apple Health + Watch is where our buyers live; resist Android until the core is proven.

---

## 10. Pricing implication

A **one-time purchase** is not a constraint — it is *the* differentiator for these buyers. The privacy/anti-subscription segment will pay a premium *because* it's one-time and private. Price with confidence as a quality, respectful alternative; don't race competitors to the bottom or toward subscriptions.
